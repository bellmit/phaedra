Well

A well is a part of a plate where substances can be added to. Traditional microtiter plates contain 96, 384 or 1536 wells, but Phaedra allows other combinations too.

For example, when processing a series of microscope slides in Phaedra, the series will be called a plate, and the slides will be called wells. If there are 5 slides, the "plate" will contain 5 "wells".

Every well has a well type, which specifies the role the well plays in the calculation process. A well that contains a compound whose activity is being studied, has the SAMPLE well type. A control well has a well type of LC (Low Control), HC (High Control), or another type of control. A well that does not contain any compound and has no role in the calculation process, has the EMPTY well type.

Wells that contain a compound may also specify the final concentration of that compound in the well. This information is used in dose-response curve fitting.

Any well may be rejected as part of the validation process. A rejected well is usually indicated by having a colored cross drawn over it. Rejected wells do not take part in calculations, and are effectively excluded from further processing.
