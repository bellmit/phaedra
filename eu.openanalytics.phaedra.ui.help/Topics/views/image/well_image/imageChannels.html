Image Channels

Below the title bar, the available image channels are listed. Image channels are defined by the Protocol Class. Each channel can be enabled or disabled individually.

A channel usually represents an image captured in a specific mode (e.g. Brightfield or Fluorescence) or an overlay created by a segmentation algorithm. By enabling multiple channels, a composite image can be obtained.

An image may have several channels:
  • Channels are all enabled and shown upon each other by default
  • If you hover a channel with your mouse, a tooltip will appear that describes the content of the channel
  • If you want to hide certain channels, click on them to toggle on or off (a circle with a red dash will appear)


Raw intensity channels have a filled icon. For line overlays, only the outline of the icon is colored.
